Why $S = 1$ models are different

  • Support on-site anisotropy $\propto S_z^2$
  • Describe class of magnetic materials
  • New topological phases (AKLT/Haldane)

A quantum simulator for $S = 1$

  • $N=2$ Mott insulator
  • Spin degree of freedom $|1,-1\rangle|1,1\rangle$ or $|1,-1\rangle|1,0\rangle$
  • Bose-Hubbard $\Leftrightarrow$ Heisenberg
  • Measure $\langle \psi|S_z=0\rangle$ using Feshbach resonance

\[\begin{aligned} \circ & = |a\rangle = |1,-1\rangle \\ \bullet & = |b\rangle = |1,1\rangle/|1,0\rangle \end{aligned}\]

Effective Heisenberg model

$\mathcal{H} = -J \sum_{i} \mathbf{S}_i\cdot\mathbf{S}_{i+1} + D \sum_i S_z^{~2}$

See Schachenmayer et al., PRA 92 041602(R)

Anisotropic physics, away from equilibrium

Create superposition state with global $\pi/2$ pulse:

\[\begin{aligned} |\psi\rangle = & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( |a\rangle - i|b\rangle \right)^{\otimes 2} \\ \xrightarrow{\mathrm{spin\,model}} & \frac{1}{2} \left( |\!\Uparrow\rangle -i\sqrt{2}|\circ\rangle - |\!\Downarrow\rangle \right) \end{aligned}\]

Two-site model

  • Hilbert space is 9D: $\left\{ |\!\Uparrow\Uparrow\rangle, |\!\Uparrow\circ\rangle, \cdots, |\!\Downarrow\Downarrow\rangle \right\}$
  • Only $\sum_i m_s = 0$ subspace couples
  • Effective Hamiltonian & basis:

    • \[ \begin{pmatrix} J + 2D & -\sqrt{2} J \\ -\sqrt{2} J & 0 \end{pmatrix} \left| \begin{matrix} \left(|\!\Uparrow\Downarrow\rangle + |\!\Downarrow\Uparrow\rangle\right)/\sqrt{2} \\ |\circ\circ\rangle \end{matrix} \right\rangle \]
